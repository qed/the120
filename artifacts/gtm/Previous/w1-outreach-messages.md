# Week-1 Outreach — Warm 25 + Founding Ambassador

*Sprint §6: "Personally message 25 warmest contacts — personal notes, not blasts" and "Text 3 families about their kid becoming a Founding Ambassador." These are scripts to personalize, not templates to paste. The whole point is that they read as one-to-one. Send from Peter, personally (text/email/DM), CASL-safe personal-relationship territory.*

---

## A. The Warm 25 — personal note

**Rule of thumb:** one specific, true, personal opening line per contact (how you know them / their kid), then the ask. Keep it to ~4 sentences. No links in the first message unless they ask — the goal is a reply, not a click.

### Core version (text / DM)
> Hey [Name] — a bit of news I wanted to share with you personally before it's public. I'm starting **The 120**: a founding cohort of 120 Toronto kids, ages 8–17, in five groups (athletes, founders, makers, scholars, givers), each building a real year-long project with mentorship and getting seriously accelerated in a subject or two. [One specific line about their kid — e.g. "Kept thinking about [kid] and the [robotics / hockey / writing] thing."] Could I grab 15 minutes to tell you about it and get your honest read?

### Warmer / closer-friend version
> [Name]! Been heads-down building something and you're one of the first people I want to tell. It's **The 120** — 120 Toronto kids, five groups, a real year-long project each, plus top-1% academic acceleration. Founding cohort starts this fall and I'm hand-picking the first families. [Specific line about their kid.] Free for a quick call this week? Would genuinely value your take.

### Email version (for contacts you email, not text)
> **Subject: something I'm building — wanted you to hear it first**
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> Quick personal note. I've started **The 120** — a founding cohort of 120 motivated Toronto kids, ages 8–17, across five groups: athletes, founders, makers, scholars, and givers. Each kid ships one real year-long project with real mentorship, and gets seriously accelerated in a subject or two (mastery-based, no ceiling). It closes on quarterly Toronto intensives where the kids demo their work.
>
> [One or two sentences specific to them / their kid.]
>
> I'm talking to the first families personally before anything goes wide. Could I steal 15 minutes this week? If it's a fit for [kid], great; if not, I'd still love your honest read.
>
> — Peter

### If they reply "tell me more"
Send the one-page explainer PDF + the booking link (cal.com/peter.k/the120), and offer two concrete times. Keep the momentum toward a call — the deposit closes on trust, and trust closes on a conversation.

### Tracking (Friday review)
Log each of the 25 in the CRM with source = warm/personal. Targets for W1: **25 warm convos started, 10 calls booked.** Anyone who books gets sent through the normal dossier → call flow.

## B. Founding Ambassador — text to 3 families

*Ambassadors = grades 7/8 kids (F1/F2) who bring friends to the Gauntlet and to info sessions. Incentive is recognition, never cash — no per-signup payments to minors, no quotas, parent consent always. This text goes to the **parent**, about their kid.*

### To the parent
> Hi [Parent] — a fun one. I'm launching **The 120** (founding cohort of 120 Toronto kids this fall) and I'm picking a small group of grade 7/8 kids to be **Founding Ambassadors**. Honestly [Kid] is exactly who I have in mind — [one true specific reason: "the kids listen to them" / "they're the connector in their grade"]. It's all recognition, no money and no pressure: named in the Address Book, a badge at the fall intensive, first pick of demo slot, early access to new levels of the math game we built. Totally opt-in and I'd loop you in on everything. Can I send you the one-pager on what it actually involves?

### The parent-facing "what it involves" note (send if they say yes)
> Here's the plain version. What [Kid] would do: challenge friends to our Gauntlet math tournament (their own referral code on the share card), bring one family to an info session or our August meetup, and maybe give a 2-minute "why I'm in" at a session — kid voice sells better than any deck. **No money changes hands, ever** — recognition only (Founding Ambassador title, intensive badge, leaderboard flair). No quotas, no pressure, opt out anytime. Everything runs with your consent, and we only ever email families who sign up themselves. That's it.

### Why these three first
Pick the three whose kid is (a) genuinely networked in grade 7/8 and (b) already likes the Gauntlet or would. One yes here seeds W2's ambassador kickoff (target: 12–15 ambassadors onboarded).
