# The 120 — Founding Ambassador Kit

*Week-2 GTM asset (sprint §2/§3). The kit an ambassador (and their parent) gets when they say yes. Recognition only — no money, no quotas, parent consent always. Grades 7/8 (F1/F2). Print the kid-facing page, text the parent note, issue the code.*

**Contents:** (1) Kid-facing one-pager · (2) Referral-code card · (3) Parent consent note · (4) Shareable images (pending Ethan's share card).

---

## 1. Kid-facing one-pager — "You're a Founding Ambassador"

### You're one of the first.

The 120 is a founding cohort of 120 Toronto kids building interesting lives together — athletes, founders, makers, scholars, givers. You're one of a small group of **Founding Ambassadors**: the kids who help build the network from the inside. Not a job, not homework — you're just one of the first, and you get to bring people with you.

### What you actually do (all optional, all fun)

- **Challenge your friends to the Gauntlet.** You've got your own code — when a friend plays from your share, that's on you. Rack up challenges.
- **Bring one friend's family** to an info session or the August meetup. One is plenty. Real beats big.
- **Say one true thing.** If you're up for it, give a 2-minute "why I'm in" at an info session. Kids listen to kids way more than they listen to any adult with slides.
- **Show off the Tin Can + Address Book.** It's a screen-free bat phone to 119 other kids. It's a genuinely cool object — let people see it.

### What you get (this is the fun part)

- **The title:** you're a **Founding Ambassador** — named in The 120 Address Book, with a badge at the **Nov 7 Demo Day**.
- **Gauntlet glory:** permanent flair on the Founding Leaderboard + early access to new bosses before anyone else.
- **First pick:** first choice of demo slot at the Nov 7 Demo Day, and a seat at the ambassador table.
- **Shout-outs:** hit a milestone (like 3 families joining because of you) and you get called out at the Demo Day.

### The honest part

No money changes hands, ever — this is about recognition, not payment. No quotas, no pressure, and you can stop anytime. Your parent is looped in on everything. We only ever email families who sign up themselves — you're the spark, not the mailing list.

## 2. Referral-code card

*A small card (physical print + digital image) each ambassador gets. Also baked into their Gauntlet share card — see the share-card design brief.*

```
┌───────────────────────────────────┐
│   THE 120 · FOUNDING AMBASSADOR    │
│                                    │
│            YOUR CODE               │
│           AMB-{FIRST}              │
│                                    │
│   Friends join at:                 │
│   the120.school/gauntlet           │
│   (your code auto-fills)           │
│                                    │
│   Recognition only · no $ · opt-in │
└───────────────────────────────────┘
```

- **Code format:** `AMB-FIRSTNAME` (e.g. `AMB-MAYA`). One per ambassador. Maps to the `AMB-NAME` referral field already live on signup.
- **How it travels:** on the printed card, in the kid's Gauntlet share card, and as a plain link `the120.school/g/AMB-MAYA` that pre-fills the code.
- **Tracking:** shows up in the CRM Source column as `A friend or ambassador` + the code. Tallied in the Friday review.

## 3. Parent consent note

*Text/email this to the parent when their kid says yes. Plain language, no hype. This is the whole agreement.*

> Hi {{parent_first}} — here's the plain version of what being a **Founding Ambassador** means for {{kid_first}}.
>
> **What they'd do:** challenge friends to our Gauntlet math tournament using their own code, maybe bring one friend's family to an info session or our August meetup, and — only if they want — give a quick 2-minute "why I'm in" at a session.
>
> **What they get:** recognition, not money. A Founding Ambassador title, a name in the Address Book, a badge and first pick of demo slot at the Nov 7 Demo Day, and some leaderboard flair in the Gauntlet.
>
> **The guardrails, in writing:** No money changes hands, ever. No quotas and no pressure — it's all opt-in and they can stop anytime. We only ever email families who sign up themselves; we never ask {{kid_first}} for anyone's contact info. You're looped in on everything.
>
> If you're good with it, just reply "yes" and I'll send {{kid_first}} their code and the kit. — Peter

**Consent logged:** keep the parent's "yes" (text/email) on file, tagged to the ambassador record. That reply is the consent.
