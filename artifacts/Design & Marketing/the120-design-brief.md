## 1. What Changed From v3

v3 sold the GT program in Toronto — a school alternative. v4 sells **membership in The 120**: a network of the 120 best and brightest students in Toronto. The GT academic machinery is still underneath, but the front door is now the network, not the school.

The three-part product a family buys:

1. **The Network** — your child joins the 120, gets a Tin Can and The 120 Address Book: a bat phone to 119 true intellectual peers across the city.
2. **The Project** — one super interesting, year-long project, mentored and shipped, demoed at the quarterly intensives.
3. **The Subject** — get super advanced in 1 academic subject (or 2), accelerated via the GT/TimeBack platform with a PhD-level Academic Advisor.

Main call to action, everywhere: **"Come join the network. Come join the 120."**

## 5. Audience

Primary: Ontario/GTA parents of high-aptitude children in **grades 3–8** — top 1–2% kids who ask for more. These parents are mostly keeping their kids in current schools (public gifted streams, $25k–$40k privates) and want acceleration AND belonging *without* blowing up their schooling. That's exactly what Membership offers — this is a bigger market than v3's school-replacement pitch.

Secondary: Ontario homeschooling families seeking a rigorous, structured academic core (→ Full Core tier).

## 6. What The 120 Is / Is Not

**Is:**
- A selective network: 120 members total, city-wide, assessment-gated (same top 1–2% bar as GT)
- One super interesting year-long project per member — real mentorship, real shipping, demoed at intensives
- Super advanced in 1 subject (or 2): AI-adaptive acceleration via TimeBack + weekly 1:1 with a PhD-level Academic Advisor
- Connected daily: every member gets a Tin Can and The 120 Address Book — screen-free, parent-approved, a bat phone to the network
- In person: four quarterly 48-hour intensives in Toronto
- 3–5 hours/week — fits around any school
- An upgrade path to the complete GT academic program for families who want it all

**Is NOT (site must actively counter):**
- NOT a tutoring service or enrichment class — it's a network and a body of real work
- NOT screen-time — the network runs on Tin Cans (voice, no screens) and in-person intensives; lead with this against the "more screens" objection
- NOT for remediation — for kids who ask for more
- NOT school replacement (at the Membership tier) — members keep their school; The 120 is what school can't give them
- NOT open enrollment — 120 seats, qualifying assessment, selective by design
- NOT the general Alpha program — that's Alpha Anywhere Canada

## 8. Voice & Tone

gt.school's high-conviction warmth, refracted through membership and scarcity. Not "enroll in a school" — **"join the 120."** The voice says: there are 120 kids in this city who need each other, and your child might be one of them.

Keep GT's signature vocabulary: "intellectual tribe," "joyful intensity," "remove the ceiling," "mastery before progression," "3x learning velocity." Add the 120's own: "the network," "your seat," "the Address Book," "the bat phone," "one project, shipped," "founding 120."

Sample headline directions (adapt, don't finalize):
- "There are 120 of them. Is your kid one?"
- "Come join the network. Come join the 120."
- "One year-long project. One subject, mastered. 119 kids who get it."
- "3–5 hours a week. A tribe for life."

Avoid: apologetic hedging, egalitarian softening, tutoring language ("support," "enrichment," "help with homework"), and anything implying it's a full-time school (Membership tier).

## 13. Conversion: Account, Dashboard & Dossier

The site is not a brochure with a form — it replaces GT's "Begin Application" handoff with a **native account + dashboard flow** on the120.school. Parents create an account, build a dossier for each child, and submit it for review. Everything stays on our domain.

### 13.1 The funnel

**Join the 120** (every CTA) → **Create account** → **Dashboard: add kids + build dossiers** → **Submit for review** → The 120 team reviews → **invited to assessment + call** → offered a seat → member.

### 13.2 Account creation (replaces "Begin Application" / "Secure Your Candidacy" modal)

Same initial fields as the GT Anywhere application:

- Parent first name, last name
- Email, phone
- Postal code (not zip)
- Household income — same 4 options as GT Anywhere: **Under $65,000 / $65,000 – $160,000 / Over $160,000 / Prefer not to say** (label currency; confirm whether brackets stay as-is or get CAD-adjusted — Open Items)
- **CASL-compliant** express opt-in checkbox for email/SMS + unsubscribe language (do not copy GT's US SMS consent text)

Account creation IS the lead capture — the moment it's submitted, the family is in the funnel and nurture email begins. Headline energy: "Claim your child's seat — Fall 2026."

**Auth & stack:** Supabase (same stack as GT's workshops tool — eases future data sharing), email + password with magic-link fallback. Parent accounts only; kids never log in.

### 13.3 Parent dashboard

After sign-up, parents land in a dashboard with:

1. **Add your kids** — one profile per child, grades 3–8; multiple kids per account.
2. **Build each dossier** (see 13.4) — a completeness meter per child drives progression.
3. **Browse workshops** — the same catalog as https://community.gt.school/workshops, rendered **natively** in the dashboard (advisor, title, description, track, grade range, session length). Parents save selections per child; selections feed the dossier. No time-voting/enrollment-ranking mechanics at this stage — that's GT's scheduling flow, ours is expression of interest. Catalog sync mechanism: Open Items.
4. **Pick subjects** — the 1–2 academic subjects each child wants to get super advanced in (Math, Reading, Writing, Science, History + free-text "other").
5. **Seat status** — where each child stands: Draft → Submitted → In review → Invited to assessment → Offered a seat → Member of the 120.

### 13.4 The dossier

Each child's dossier is the application artifact — designed to feel like a candidate profile for the 120, not a form. Contents:

- **Basics:** name, grade, birth year, current school, optional photo
- **Academic picks:** the 1–2 subjects + any test scores/assessments the parent wants to share (MAP, CCAT, report cards — optional uploads)
- **Workshop selections:** saved from the catalog
- **Project pitch & interests:** the kid's interests, a proposed year-long project idea (free text, kid's own words encouraged), achievements/portfolio links

The dossier renders as a polished one-page profile (on-brand, printable/PDF-able) — parents should *want* to complete it because the artifact itself is compelling. When complete, **Submit for review** sends it to The 120 admissions team; the parent gets a confirmation and the review → assessment invitation → call sequence begins.

### 13.5 Data model (for the builder)

`parents` (account + income bracket + CASL consent) → `children` (basics) → `subject_picks`, `workshop_selections`, `project_pitch`, `uploads` → `dossier` (assembled view + status). Admin view for the team: dossier queue, status changes, notes.

### 13.6 Secondary conversion

- **Book a Call** — persistent secondary CTA; also where the Full Core upgrade conversation happens. Available pre- and post-account.
- Nurture email (CASL-consented): welcome, dossier-completion nudges, submission confirmation, status updates, intensive dates.
- Contact: admissions@the120.school (placeholder — confirm); phone TBD.

5. Never imply a Toronto campus exists today.
